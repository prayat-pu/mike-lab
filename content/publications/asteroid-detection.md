# Content
abstract: "Detecting small, fast-moving asteroids in long-exposure astronomical images is a 'needle-in-a-haystack' problem. We propose a self-supervised contrastive learning architecture that learns to distinguish astronomical motion from sensor noise and cosmic rays without requiring large-scale manual annotations, significantly accelerating the pipeline for planetary defense."

### Summary of Contribution
By applying temporal knowledge graphs (TeReKG) to link sequential exposures, our architecture successfully identified three previously uncatalogued near-Earth objects during our validation phase at the regional observatory.
